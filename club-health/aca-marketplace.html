What Are ACA Plans?

Health insurance through the Marketplace — often with subsidy assistance.

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) created federal and state Health Insurance Marketplaces where individuals and families can purchase comprehensive health coverage. Depending on your income, you may qualify for a Premium Tax Credit (PTC) that significantly reduces what you pay each month.

All ACA plans must cover essential health benefits — including preventive care, emergency services, hospitalization, prescription drugs, and mental health services — and cannot deny coverage for pre-existing conditions.

Emily's job is to find the plan that gives you the right coverage at the right price, and to make sure you're receiving every subsidy dollar you're entitled to.

ACA Metal Tiers at a Glance

🥉 Bronze Lowest premium / highest out-of-pocket

Plan pays ~60% of costs. Good if you're young and healthy and mainly need catastrophic protection.

🥈 Silver Best for subsidy-eligible individuals

Plan pays ~70%. Cost-Sharing Reductions (CSR) are only available on Silver plans — making them often the best value for those who qualify.

🥇 Gold Higher premium / lower out-of-pocket

Plan pays ~80%. Great if you use medical care frequently and want predictable costs.

💎 Platinum Highest premium / lowest out-of-pocket

Plan pays ~90%. Best for people with frequent medical needs who want maximum coverage.

Enrollment Periods

When you can sign up for ACA coverage

Open Enrollment Period

The main window to enroll in or change Marketplace coverage each year. Typically runs November 1 through January 15 for most states (federal Marketplace). Coverage begins January 1 if you enroll by December 15.

Special Enrollment Period (SEP)

Certain life events let you enroll outside Open Enrollment. Qualifying events include:

  • → Losing employer health coverage
  • → Getting married or divorced
  • → Having or adopting a child
  • → Moving to a new coverage area
  • → Losing Medicaid or CHIP eligibility
