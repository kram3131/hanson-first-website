What Is Final Expense Insurance?

Small coverage. Significant peace of mind.

Final expense insurance (also called burial insurance or funeral insurance) is a type of whole life policy designed to cover end-of-life costs. Coverage amounts typically range from $5,000 to $25,000 — enough to cover funeral expenses, medical bills, and other costs that can burden a grieving family.

Unlike larger life insurance policies, final expense plans are designed specifically for seniors and often feature:

✓
Simplified enrollment

Many plans require only a few health questions — no full medical exam required.

✓
Guaranteed acceptance options

Some plans are available regardless of your health history, though premiums are higher.

✓
Fixed premiums for life

Your premium is locked in when you enroll and never increases, even as you age.

✓
Cash payout to beneficiaries

Your family receives a tax-free lump sum they can use however they need — funeral costs, outstanding bills, or anything else.

Plan Options

Types of final expense coverage

Simplified Issue

Requires answers to a few health questions but no full medical exam. If you're in reasonably good health, this typically offers the best premiums. Coverage begins immediately.

Best Rates

Graded Benefit

For those with more significant health conditions. Full benefits may not be payable in the first 2–3 years (a graded or waiting period applies), but the policy can still be obtained.

For Health Conditions

Guaranteed Issue

No health questions. Cannot be denied coverage. Premiums are higher and a waiting period applies, but it ensures everyone can get at least some coverage regardless of health history.
