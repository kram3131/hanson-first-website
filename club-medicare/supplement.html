What is Medigap?

It fills the 20% gap Original Medicare doesn't cover.

Medicare Supplement (Medigap) policies are sold by private insurers and work alongside Original Medicare (Parts A & B). They help cover out-of-pocket costs like coinsurance, copayments, and deductibles.

Unlike Medicare Advantage, a Supplement plan doesn't replace Original Medicare — it works with it. This means you can see any doctor in the country who accepts Medicare without worrying about network restrictions.

Medigap also offers budget predictability. Because the policy covers most of your cost-sharing, you know roughly what your medical expenses will look like each month.

Supplement vs. Advantage

Which is right for you?

Choose Medigap if you…

  • ✓ Want to see any Medicare-accepting doctor
  • ✓ Travel frequently or split time between states
  • ✓ Have frequent medical appointments
  • ✓ Want predictable out-of-pocket costs
  • ✓ Don't need extra benefits like dental/vision

Choose Medicare Advantage if you…

  • → Want extra benefits like dental, vision, and hearing
  • → Are comfortable staying within a provider network
  • → Want a lower monthly premium
  • → Live in one geographic area year-round

Enrollment timing is critical for Medigap

The best time to enroll in a Medigap policy is during your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period — which begins the month you're 65 and enrolled in Part B. During this window, insurers cannot deny coverage or charge more due to pre-existing conditions. After this window, medical underwriting may apply.
