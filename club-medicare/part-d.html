How Part D Works

Prescription drug coverage for Medicare beneficiaries.

Medicare Part D adds prescription drug coverage to Original Medicare. It's offered through private insurance companies approved by Medicare. Without Part D coverage, you're responsible for 100% of your prescription drug costs — and the late enrollment penalty can follow you for life.

Emily reviews your specific medications against each plan's formulary (drug list) to find the plan that gives you the best coverage at the lowest cost. Every prescription matters.

Standalone PDP vs. MAPD

Standalone PDP

A Part D plan used alongside Original Medicare or a Medigap policy. You have two separate plans — one for medical, one for drugs.

MAPD (Medicare Advantage + Drug)

Drug coverage bundled into a Medicare Advantage plan. One plan, one card. Great if you're already considering Medicare Advantage. Learn about Advantage →

Key Enrollment Rules

When and how to enroll in Part D

🎂

Initial Enrollment Period

You can enroll in Part D during your 7-month window around your 65th birthday (3 months before, the month of, and 3 months after).

📅

Annual Enrollment Period

Oct 15 – Dec 7 each year. You can switch, drop, or add Part D coverage. Changes take effect January 1.

⚠️

Late Enrollment Penalty

If you don't enroll in Part D when first eligible and don't have other qualifying drug coverage, you'll pay a lifetime penalty added to your monthly premium. Don't miss your window.

2026 Highlight: The $2,100 Out-of-Pocket Cap

Starting in 2025 and fully phased in by 2026, Medicare beneficiaries have a $2,100 annual cap on out-of-pocket drug costs — a landmark change from the Inflation Reduction Act. This makes Part D coverage more valuable than ever. See all 2026 updates →
