Key 2026 Changes

What's new in Medicare for 2026

💊

$2,000 Part D Out-of-Pocket Cap

The Inflation Reduction Act established a $2,000 annual cap on Medicare Part D out-of-pocket drug costs. This is a landmark change — there was previously no cap. If you take expensive medications, this could save you thousands per year.

Ongoing IRA Benefit
Inflation Reduction Act

How the IRA is changing Medicare drug costs.

The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) introduced several significant changes to Medicare prescription drug coverage that are being phased in through 2026 and beyond:

✓
Vaccine coverage — $0 cost sharing

Recommended vaccines covered under Part D now have $0 cost sharing for Medicare beneficiaries.

✓
$35 insulin cap

All covered insulin products under Medicare Part D are capped at $35/month.

✓
$2,000 annual out-of-pocket cap

The catastrophic phase of Part D is eliminated, replaced by a $2,000 annual cap.

✓
Medicare drug price negotiation

For the first time, Medicare is negotiating prices directly with drug manufacturers for select high-cost drugs.

Enrollment Calendar

When you can make changes to your Medicare coverage

Oct 15 – Dec 7

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

The main window to switch, drop, or join Medicare Advantage or Part D plans. Changes take effect January 1. This is the most important enrollment window for most people.

Jan 1 – Mar 31

Open Enrollment Period (OEP)

Medicare Advantage enrollees can switch to a different MA plan or return to Original Medicare (and join a Part D plan). You cannot switch from Original Medicare to MA during OEP.

3 mo. before 65 – 3 mo. after

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

Your first chance to sign up for Medicare. The 7-month window around your 65th birthday. Enrolling on time is critical to avoid lifetime penalties.

Year-round

Special Enrollment Periods (SEP)

Triggered by qualifying life events — moving, losing employer coverage, becoming dual eligible, and more. If you've had a major life change, you may be able to make changes outside standard windows.
