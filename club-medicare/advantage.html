What is Medicare Advantage?

Part C — all-in-one coverage from private insurers.

Medicare Advantage (Part C) is an alternative to Original Medicare offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare. Instead of getting your Medicare benefits directly from the federal government, you receive them through the private plan.

Most Medicare Advantage plans include hospital (Part A), medical (Part B), and often prescription drug (Part D) coverage all in one plan — sometimes for little to no monthly premium beyond what you already pay for Part B.

Types of Medicare Advantage

Finding the plan that fits your situation

🏥

HMO Plans

Use a network of doctors and hospitals. Usually require a primary care physician (PCP) and referrals for specialists. Typically the lowest-premium option.

🗺️

PPO Plans

More flexibility to see any Medicare-approved provider. No referral needed. Better for people who travel or want broader provider access.

❤️

Special Needs Plans (SNP)

Tailored for people with chronic conditions (C-SNP) or dual Medicare/Medicaid eligibility (D-SNP). Learn more →

💊

Medicare Advantage with Drug Coverage (MAPD)

Many Medicare Advantage plans bundle Part D prescription drug coverage — called MAPD. This means one plan, one card, one premium. Emily can help you evaluate whether a bundled MAPD or a standalone PDP makes more sense for your prescriptions.
